What Is Student Life Like at High Point University?

High Point University is a residential campus where most students live, eat, attend concerts, and go to the movies without leaving the property. This is a guide to what is actually here.

The Short Answer

What Does Student Life at HPU Look Like?

HPU has put more than $1 billion into the campus since 2005, all of it for the students who live and learn here. State-of-the-art academic facilities, fine-dining restaurants on the meal plan, an arena that books national touring acts, and free events nearly every week. More than 6,500 students are enrolled, and 95% of them live on campus.

Common Questions About Life at HPU

HPU students have access to a movie theater, arcade, six outdoor heated pools, a 5,000-seat arena that hosts national touring artists, and free weekly events through the Campus Activities Team, all on campus.
Most Campus Activities Team events are free, including weekly food trucks. Concerts at the Qubein Arena, movies at the Extraordinaire Cinema, and recreational facilities are included in enrollment.
Artists who have performed at the Qubein Arena for students include J. Cole, Maren Morris, Jason Derulo, T-Pain, and Flo Rida.
Princeton Review ranks HPU’s residence halls second in the nation. About 95% of students live on campus.
HPU has more than 20 on-campus dining options, including three fine-dining restaurants where students can use a meal swipe.
