Entrepreneur in Residence

Marc Randolph

Co-Founder & First CEO, Netflix

Marc Randolph co-founded Netflix and served as its first CEO — and as High Point University’s Entrepreneur in Residence, he teaches our students that the only way to know whether an idea is good is to test it.

A serial entrepreneur and author of “That Will Never Work,” Randolph has built and advised dozens of companies. His method is relentlessly practical: try the idea quickly, learn from what breaks, and try again. Through HPU’s Access to Innovators Program, he brings that hard-won candor straight to student founders.

For HPU students, Randolph is a living lesson in the Premier Life Skills of adaptability and resilience. He teaches that rejection is data, that certainty is overrated, and that the willingness to start before you feel ready is the entrepreneur’s real advantage.

On Campus at HPU

What Marc Randolph Teaches HPU Students

Most entrepreneurship courses teach frameworks. Through HPU’s Access to Innovators Program, Randolph brings something rarer: the unfiltered experience of building a company from nothing to Netflix, including every wrong turn along the way. HPU students get his honest version of the story — which is far more useful than the polished one.

01

Entrepreneurship intensives

Randolph works directly with student founders to turn an instinct into a small, testable experiment. He moves fast, asks hard questions, and gives the kind of honest feedback most students never get until it’s too late.

02

The art of the pitch

He coaches students to communicate an idea with enough clarity and conviction that someone else will bet time or money on it. It’s a skill that transfers into every career, from the job interview to the boardroom.

03

Resilience through no

Randolph teaches students that rejection is the ordinary cost of trying. His sessions give them the mental model to keep going when the data says stop.
