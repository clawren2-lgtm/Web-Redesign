Broadcaster in Residence

Joe Michaels

Former Director, NBC’s “Today” Show

Joe Michaels directed NBC’s “Today” show for more than two decades — and as High Point University’s Broadcaster in Residence, he teaches our students the practical mechanics of presence.

A veteran broadcast director and author of “Hit Your Mark and Say Your Lines,” Michaels has shaped how millions of people experience live television and has coached presidents, CEOs, and anchors on how to command a room and a camera. At HPU he also serves as a professor of practice of communication.

Through HPU’s Access to Innovators Program, Michaels gives students a rare gift: the Premier Life Skills of communication, coachability, and composure that turn a nervous speaker into someone an audience wants to follow.

On Campus at HPU

What Joe Michaels Teaches HPU Students

Joe Michaels has spent a career deciding in real time whether someone commands a room or loses it. That judgment is rare, and through HPU’s Access to Innovators Program he brings it directly to students as hands-on coaching that produces visible, measurable results before they leave the room.

01

On-camera and on-stage presence

Michaels runs hands-on sessions where students actually get in front of a camera. He gives direct, in-the-moment coaching on posture, eye contact, pace, and the subtle things that tell an audience whether to trust you.

02

Executive communication

He coaches students to deliver a message the way leaders do — with clarity, authority, and the confidence to hold a room. These are skills that transfer from the boardroom to the job interview to the first day on the job.

03

Coachability under pressure

Michaels is known for direct, honest feedback. His sessions teach students to receive it well, apply it immediately, and improve fast — the habit that separates people who grow from people who plateau.
