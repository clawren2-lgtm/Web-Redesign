High Point University competes in 17 NCAA Division I sports, most of them in the Big South Conference, and has built one of the most decorated mid-major athletic departments in the country. The Panthers have won NCAA Tournament games in four different sports: women’s lacrosse, men’s soccer, baseball, and men’s basketball, whose 12-seed beat Wisconsin for the program’s first NCAA Tournament win.

Men’s Basketball

A 12-seed beat Wisconsin on a layup with 11.7 seconds left.

On March 19, 2026, the 12th-seeded Panthers beat 5-seed Wisconsin 83–82 at the Moda Center in Portland. It was the first NCAA Tournament win in program history and the first time High Point had beaten a Power Four opponent, decided by a go-ahead layup with 11.7 seconds left. The Panthers finished 31–5, won a third straight Big South regular-season title, and took back-to-back Big South Tournament championships in 2025 and 2026. Home games fill the 4,500-seat Nido and Mariana Qubein Arena and Conference Center, and the student section is loud when it matters.

Women’s Basketball

The best stretch in the program’s Division I history, and still climbing.

High Point women’s basketball finished 27–6 and reached the NCAA Tournament for a second straight year, the best stretch in the program’s Division I history. The Panthers have won the Big South regular-season title three years running and hold back-to-back conference tournament championships, the most recent a 71–67 win over Radford. They play in the same arena as the men, and the program is still climbing.

The Venues

High Point built the venues to match the programs. Basketball plays in the 4,500-seat Nido and Mariana Qubein Arena and Conference Center, opened in 2021. How can students play football at High Point University?

Yes, through intramurals. Flag football is one of the most popular intramural sports at HPU, run through HPU REC alongside dodgeball, ultimate frisbee, and indoor volleyball. Beyond intramurals, more than 750 students compete on over 30 Intercollegiate Club Sports teams. Club hockey has won an ACCHL championship and reached an AAU national title game, the women’s club hockey team won its third ACCHL title in February 2026, and the club esports team won the inaugural Big South Esports Championship in 2024. There is no varsity football, and there is still a field to play on most weeks of the fall.
