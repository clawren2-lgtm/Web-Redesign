What It Is

What emotional intelligence is made of

Emotional intelligence is the ability to recognize and manage your own emotions, and to read and respond to the emotions of others. Psychologists describe it through five distinct, teachable abilities, each one HPU develops in every student.

Self-Awareness

Recognizing your own emotions as they happen, and knowing how they shape your decisions and the people around you.

Self-Regulation

Managing impulses and staying composed under pressure, so your response is a choice rather than a reaction.

Motivation

The internal drive to pursue goals with energy and persistence, the engine behind an attitude of excellence.

Empathy

Reading what others feel and standing in their shoes, the foundation of trust, teamwork, and real leadership.

Social Skills

Managing relationships, navigating conflict, and communicating clearly to turn emotional insight into action.

Framework drawn from the Salovey & Mayer and Goleman models of emotional intelligence · HPU Emotional Intelligence research guide
