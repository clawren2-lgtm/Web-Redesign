Talent Acquisition Expert in Residence

Dee Ann Turner

Former VP of Talent, Chick-fil-A

Dee Ann Turner spent 33 years building one of the most admired cultures in American business — and as High Point University’s Talent Acquisition Expert in Residence, she teaches our students how extraordinary talent is found, developed, and kept.

Selected as Chick-fil-A’s first female officer in 2001, Turner served as Vice President of Talent and later Vice President of Sustainability, working alongside founder S. Truett Cathy as an architect of the company’s culture. Over her career she was responsible for the selection of thousands of franchisees and corporate staff.

Today she leads Dee Ann Turner & Associates, is a three-time bestselling author of It’s My Pleasure, Bet on Talent, and Crush Your Career, and hosts the Crush Your Career podcast. Through HPU’s Access to Innovators Program, she coaches students on the Premier Life Skills that separate the extraordinary employee from the ordinary: interviewing, service, character, and relational capital.

On Campus at HPU

What Dee Ann Turner Teaches HPU Students

Every student will sit on the other side of an interview table one day — and most will lead a team eventually. Through HPU’s Access to Innovators Program, Turner draws on three decades of hiring and culture-building at Chick-fil-A to show students what employers actually look for, and how to become the kind of person they fight to keep.

01

How extraordinary talent gets hired

Turner personally selected thousands of people at Chick-fil-A. She teaches students what hiring managers really weigh, how to tell their own story, and how to ace the interview that lands the job.

02

Winning the hearts of customers

Chick-fil-A taught her that “we’re not in the chicken business, we’re in the people business.” She works with students on the service mindset and relational skills that turn ordinary transactions into lasting loyalty.

03

Building a culture of care

Drawing on the systems she built with Truett Cathy, Turner shows students how trust, character, and teamwork turn a group of employees into a remarkable organization.
