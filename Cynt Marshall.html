Sports Executive in Residence

Cynt Marshall

Former CEO, Dallas Mavericks

Cynt Marshall spent 36 years at AT&T, rising to Senior Vice President and Chief Diversity Officer before becoming CEO of the Dallas Mavericks, where she led a full organizational transformation that became a studied model for workplace culture in professional sports.

As Sports Executive in Residence at High Point University, Marshall works directly with students on the leadership competencies that define long careers: emotional intelligence, values-based decision making, and the relational skills that determine how far talent actually takes you. Her message to HPU students is direct — the technical skills get you in the room. How you lead, communicate, and treat people determines everything that happens after.

What Cynt Marshall Teaches HPU Students

Cynt Marshall reached the top of her field and transformed the organization she inherited. Through HPU’s Access to Innovators Program, she works directly with students who will one day set the tone for the teams, companies, and communities around them. Her focus is the gap between talented and trusted.

01

Values-driven leadership workshops

Marshall leads hands-on sessions on guiding teams through change without losing your principles. Students leave with a personal leadership framework they can use the next day.

02

Authenticity in the workplace

She helps students bring their full identity into professional rooms with confidence and credibility — and shows why the organizations that let them do that perform better.

03

The hard conversation

Her sessions model the emotional intelligence to address a problem directly and kindly — the skill most leaders avoid developing until it costs them.
