Innovator in Residence

Steve Wozniak

Co-Founder, Apple

Steve Wozniak co-founded Apple and hand-designed the machines that made computing personal — and as High Point University’s Innovator in Residence, he brings that same curiosity directly to our students.

The sole designer of the Apple I and Apple II, Wozniak launched the personal-computing revolution by pairing technical genius with a simple conviction: technology should be honest, approachable, and built to delight the person using it. In March 2016, he became the founding voice of HPU’s Access to Innovators Program.

Through Access to Innovators, HPU students sit across from the people who changed the world and ask them anything. Wozniak models the Premier Life Skills that outlast any single technology: the judgment to know what is worth building, the integrity to build it honestly, and the curiosity to keep learning long after the world calls you an expert.

On Campus at HPU

What Steve Wozniak Teaches HPU Students

Most students will only ever read about the people who changed the world. Through HPU’s Access to Innovators Program, our students sit across from one of them and ask anything they want. Wozniak comes back to campus because he believes the next generation of builders deserves real access to the people who have done it.

01

Fireside conversations on invention

Wozniak sits down with students in small-group sessions and speaks candidly about building, failing, and the ethics of creating technology people trust. Every session is an open, honest conversation where students set the agenda.

02

Direct mentorship for student innovators

Engineering and entrepreneurship students get face-to-face feedback on the ideas and prototypes they’re actually working on. Wozniak has a rare gift for seeing the idea inside the rough prototype.

03

Judgment in the age of AI

He pushes students past “Can we build it?” to the question that defines a good engineer: “Should we?” In a world where AI can do more and more, that judgment is the skill that sets people apart.
