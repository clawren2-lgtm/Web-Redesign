Why Life Skills Matter More in an AI Economy

What HPU Said in 2005.  What the World Is Saying Now.

In 2005, President Qubein told his first incoming class they were not being prepared for the world as it is. They were being prepared for the world as it is going to be. He did not know that twenty years later the World Economic Forum would name the skills gap the number one barrier to business transformation, or that the CEOs of JPMorgan Chase and Microsoft would say the same thing in nearly the same language. He built the curriculum anyway.

HPU
2005

Nido R. Qubein becomes the seventh president of High Point University and requires every incoming freshman to take a Life Skills course he teaches personally. Communication, leadership, fiscal literacy, ethical judgment, and relational capital. No other sitting university president in the country does this.

2013

HPU opens 1924 Prime in the R.G. Wanek Center as a fine dining restaurant and learning lab. Students dine weekly in business attire, without a phone, served by staff trained to teach professional etiquette at the table. Four years of experience in a setting that mirrors the professional world they are preparing to enter.

2015

Cottrell Hall opens, bringing career development, internships, entrepreneurship, and student success coaching together under one roof so students can connect every experience they accumulate to what comes next.

2021

The Center for Community Engagement launches, connecting HPU students to the city of High Point through structured service and scholarship programs. Students develop the capacity to lead and work alongside people whose circumstances differ from their own.

2025

HPU launches tuition-free microcredentials in networking, business etiquette, coachability, and leadership, each verified and resume-ready before graduation. The Class of 2025 graduates with a 99% career placement rate, first in North Carolina.

The World
2017

Harvard economist David Deming publishes “The Growing Importance of Social Skills in the Labor Market” in the Quarterly Journal of Economics. Social-skill-intensive employment grew by nearly 12 percentage points as a share of the U.S. labor force between 1980 and 2012. Math-intensive but less social occupations shrank.

2024

MIT economist David Autor publishes NBER Working Paper 32140. AI extends human expertise rather than replacing it. The workers who benefit most are the ones who bring the judgment to direct it.

2025

Jamie Dimon, CEO of JPMorgan Chase: “Learn your EQ, learn how to be good in a meeting, how to communicate, how to write.” Satya Nadella, CEO of Microsoft: “If you just have IQ without EQ, it’s just a waste of IQ.” Deloitte’s 2026 Global Human Capital Trends names competitive advantage as less about technology and more about cultivating the human edge.

2026

The Brookings Institution and GovAI find 6.1 million U.S. workers at meaningful AI displacement risk. The differentiator: transferable, broadly developed human skills.

The world is looking for somewhere to learn these skills. HPU has been the answer for twenty years.

Frequently Asked Questions

Life Skills at High Point University

What is the Life Skills program at High Point University?
HPU’s Life Skills program is a required four-year curriculum built into every undergraduate degree. It begins with EXP 1101, the President’s Seminar on Life Skills a one-credit course President Nido Qubein teaches personally to every incoming freshman, every fall, since 2006. It continues through eight named skill categories and eleven tuition-free microcredentials earned across four years. The research behind it: HPU’s 2022 C-Suite Survey of 503 executives found that 89% of new-hire success and failure factors are Life Skills related. The outcome: 99% of the Class of 2025 employed or in graduate school within six months. See the President’s Seminar and the full credentials page.
What are the eight Life Skills HPU teaches?
HPU organizes Life Skills into eight named categories: Emotional Intelligence, Coachability, Communication and Relational Capital, Character and Integrity, Growth Mindset, Adaptability and Resilience, Leadership and Persuasion, and Judgment and Critical Thinking. Each has its own coursework, its own credential, and its own spoke page with the full depth on why that skill matters and how HPU teaches it.
Why do Life Skills matter more in an AI economy?
Because AI makes technical work more efficient, which makes human capabilities more valuable, not less. MIT economist David Autor’s 2024 NBER research shows AI extends human expertise to more workers those who benefit most bring the judgment to direct it. A January 2026 Brookings Institution and GovAI study found 6.1 million workers at meaningful AI displacement risk whose primary vulnerability is low adaptive capacity, not low technical skill. David Deming’s 2017 Quarterly Journal of Economics research documented that social-skill-intensive employment grew by nearly 12 percentage points as a share of the U.S. labor force between 1980 and 2012. HPU built the curriculum around these findings in 2006. Related: What Skills AI Cannot Replace Is College Worth It in the AI Economy?
Does President Qubein really teach a class to every freshman?
Yes. President Nido R. Qubein teaches EXP 1101, the President’s Seminar on Life Skills, a required one-credit course every incoming freshman takes in their first semester. He is the only sitting university president in the United States who teaches a required, credit-bearing course to the full entering class. Every fall, since 2006. The course covers self-esteem, goal-setting, fiscal literacy, communication, leadership, ethical decision-making, and what he calls relational capital. Full detail on the President’s Seminar.
What are HPU’s eleven Life Skills microcredentials?
HPU awards eleven tuition-free microcredentials students can earn during their undergraduate degree: Emotional Intelligence, Coachability, Motivation, Foundational Life Skills (first-year exclusive, earned alongside EXP 1101), Professional Assessment, Guest Relations (earned at the Masters Tournament in Augusta, Georgia), Global Showroom Merchandising (earned at the High Point Furniture Market), Networking and Workplace Etiquette (earned through HPU in the City at Goldman Sachs, Bloomberg, and JPMorgan Chase), Executive Sports Leadership (earned in the Dallas Mavericks boardroom), Organization Solutions, and Leadership Essentials. Every credential is tuition-free, included in the undergraduate degree, and searchable by employer applicant tracking systems. Full credentials page.
What is HPU’s career placement rate?
99% of HPU’s Class of 2025 was employed or enrolled in graduate school within 180 days of graduation, verified to NACE First-Destination Survey standards, ranked first in North Carolina, thirteen points above the national average. HPU tracked outcomes for 84% of its graduating class the highest knowledge rate published by any institution in the country. Full career outcomes data, employer destinations, and NACE methodology.
How is HPU’s Life Skills program different from career services at other universities?
At most universities, career readiness lives in an office students visit when they choose to. At HPU, it lives in the curriculum every student is required to complete a four-year, credit-bearing program with eight named pillars, a presidential seminar in the first semester, and eleven formal microcredentials. Princeton Review ranks HPU’s career services ninth nationally in student surveys. That ranking sits on top of a curriculum that has already done the foundational work before you walk into a career center appointment for the first time. Related: What Makes a Graduate Trustworthy to Employers.
Can Life Skills actually be learned in college?
Yes. The entire program is built on the premise that these capabilities are teachable, not innate. HPU’s C-Suite Survey shows executives observe Life Skills as qualities that vary meaningfully across graduates which means they respond to education, not just personality. Coachability, emotional intelligence, communication under pressure, and professional judgment all develop through instruction, practice, and honest feedback. HPU built a four-year curriculum and a credentialing system around exactly this. The 99% placement rate for the Class of 2025 is what that produces at scale.
