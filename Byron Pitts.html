Journalist in Residence

Byron Pitts

Co-Anchor, ABC’s “Nightline”

Byron Pitts couldn’t read until he was twelve and battled a stutter into adulthood — then became one of the most decorated journalists in American television and, today, High Point University’s Journalist in Residence.

Co-anchor of ABC’s “Nightline” and a multiple Emmy and Murrow award winner, Pitts is the author of “Step Out on Nothing.” His career is built on the discipline of telling true stories well — and the courage to keep going when the odds say stop. He joined HPU’s Access to Innovators Program in 2018.

Pitts teaches HPU students that communication is a learnable craft and that perseverance is a choice. He models the Premier Life Skills at the heart of an HPU education: the empathy that lets a storyteller connect, and the character that earns an audience’s trust.

On Campus at HPU

What Byron Pitts Teaches HPU Students

Byron Pitts didn’t start with an advantage. He built one — word by word, story by story, over decades of discipline. When he works with HPU students, he makes one thing clear: the ability to communicate with clarity and conviction is available to anyone willing to develop it.

01

The craft of storytelling

Pitts coaches students to find the human truth inside any story and tell it with clarity, economy, and empathy. He draws on decades of covering war, politics, and the moments that define a generation.

02

Communicating under pressure

He shares exactly how he overcame a severe stutter to speak live on national television — and gives students practical tools to do what they think they cannot.

03

Character as a competitive advantage

His sessions connect personal integrity to professional credibility — showing students that the reporter, the leader, and the colleague people trust most are always the ones who refuse to cut corners.
